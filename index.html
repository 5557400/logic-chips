First — what is a logic gate?

Think of a logic gate as a tiny box with one or more inputs and a single output. Here's the key idea: every input, and the output, can only ever be one of two states — on or off (written 1 or 0, sometimes called “high” and “low”). The box follows one fixed rule to turn its inputs into its output. That's the whole concept. Each chip below simply packs several identical gates onto one fingernail-sized sliver of silicon.

Each black chip holds several identical gates. Inside the chip you'll see the gate symbols light up as signals flow through. Pins 7 and 14 aren't for logic — they just feed the chip electricity (Ground and +5 volts).
